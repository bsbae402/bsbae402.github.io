Who am I?

I am Bongsung Bae.

I am an SBU computer science student who is interested in Linux/Windows system programming, data science, machine learning, and computer game development. I am from South Korea.

These days, computer programming is not a hard thing to learn, thanks to the internet. However, I know that knowing how to code does not directly lead to building a reliable and beautifully coded software. Through the numerous undergraduate projects, I learned that a great computer program can only be made when you really love, enjoy, and be passionate about what you are creating.

It is even more difficult if you build a massive program as a team. I have been a team member for some undergraduate projects; I was a part of 2D windows game, 3D game on Unity engine, and speech-recognizable JEOPARDY! quiz builder projects. The language barrier between me and my team had caused some difficulty at early stages of the projects, but my teams always managed to go through really well. All of the projects got A grades.

As a programmer, I have confidence in using C, C++, and Java programming languages. I also have some experience with JavaScript, along with HTML5, CSS, jQuery, and JSON. I have used Python with NumPy, scikit-learn for data analysis in Statistics courses, but the level of proficiency in the language remains at the basics.

I am a passionate computer gamer, and hobby guitarist.

My code of conduct on coding

  • Don’t type in if you don’t know what you are doing.
  • If the logic is complicated, get a pen and draw the flow on a paper.
  • Don’t be rushed. For that, manage the schedule reasonably.
  • If you are not familiar with the tool, make some small projects for practice. Don’t do experiments on your working project!
  • If the part currently being coded should be changed or deleted in the future, make them distinctive and stood out.
